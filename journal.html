📜 The Archive Keeper's Journal

Hidden among the shelves of the Midnight Archive are the personal notes of its keeper—observations on myths, immortality, forgotten stories, and the questions that refuse to die.

🦇 Entry I — Why Do Vampires Endure?

Recorded: Under a moonless sky.

Every generation seems to reinvent the vampire. Sometimes a monster, sometimes a tragedy, sometimes a mirror of human fear. But the truth is deeper: vampires endure because we never stop asking what immortality would cost.

🌙 Entry II — The Sound of Empty Castles

Recorded: During a silent night in the Archive.

There is a sound that old castles are said to make when no one is watching them. Not wind. Not wood. Something slower… like memory stretching through stone.

🧩 Entry III — Riddles Are Not Games

Recorded: After solving an unsolved question.

A riddle is not meant to confuse—it is meant to reveal how you think. Every answer says more about the solver than the question.

🐺 Entry IV — The Werewolf Incident

Recorded: October 17, 1887

Reports arrived from the northern forests three nights ago. Livestock torn apart. Tracks too large for any ordinary wolf. The villagers refuse to speak after sundown.

One hunter swears he saw a man collapse to all fours beneath the full moon. Another insists the creature stood taller than any human and moved with impossible speed.

I dismissed their stories as superstition.

I no longer do.

The western corridor remains sealed. I still hear movement behind the fourth door.
